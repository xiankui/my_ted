# You Are Not the Center of the Universe

Given our ego-centric nature, we often suffer from the "Spotlight Effect." We tend to believe that everyone is noticing our flaws or mistakes. However, the truth is quite different.

In terms of social psychology, most people are too busy worrying about their own "spotlights" to notice yours. It all comes down to the fact that we are the protagonists of our own movies, but only background extras in others'. By realizing this, we can get away from social anxiety and start being ourselves. After all, freedom is the name of the game.

## 1. 核心骨架 (The Core)

文中有一句：“We tend to believe that everyone is noticing our flaws or mistakes.”

骨架： We tend to believe something. (我们倾向于相信某些事)

内容： Everyone is noticing our flaws. (每个人都在注意到我们的缺陷)

## 2. 逻辑锚点与核能动词

Given our ego-centric nature：考虑到我们“以自我为中心”的天性。（设定心理学背景）

Filter out (虽然这篇没直接用，但逻辑相通)：别人会自动“过滤掉”关于你的无关细节。

Protagonists vs. Extras：主角 vs. 群众演员。（形象的对比）
